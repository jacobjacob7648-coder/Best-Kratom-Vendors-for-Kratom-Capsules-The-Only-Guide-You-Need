## Why Capsule Quality Matters More Than You Think

The capsule format introduces three quality variables that powder buyers don't face:

**Fill Weight Accuracy** — Stated fill weight (typically 0.5g or 0.7g per capsule) must match actual fill weight. Variance of even 0.1g per capsule creates 20% dosing error — significant for users managing specific effects.

**Powder Freshness at Time of Filling** — Capsules lock in powder quality at filling. Old powder in a new capsule is still old powder. The best vendors fill capsules from recent harvests, not aged inventory.

**Shell Integrity** — Capsule shells must be intact and sealed consistently. Moisture-compromised or improperly sealed capsules degrade faster and absorb inconsistently.

Research in *Drug and Alcohol Dependence* found that dosing consistency significantly impacts user outcomes with kratom — making accurate capsule fill weights more than a quality-of-life issue.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long do kratom capsules take to work?**
25–45 minutes for initial effects. Longer than powder because the shell must dissolve first. Take on a light stomach for faster onset.

**Are vegetable cellulose capsules better than gelatin?**
Neither is inherently better for kratom effects. Cellulose is vegan-friendly and may dissolve slightly faster. Both work well.

**Can I make my own capsules at home?**
Yes. Capsule-filling machines and empty shells are widely available. Cost-effective if you already buy quality powder in bulk.

**How should I store kratom capsules?**
Cool, dark, airtight container. Avoid refrigeration — condensation moisture damages shells and degrades powder. Properly stored capsules last 1–2 years.

**What does AKA GMP Certified mean for capsules?**
It means the filling facility meets American Kratom Association Good Manufacturing Practice standards — covering cleanliness, equipment, testing protocols, and quality control. Verify at americankratom.org.
